1. Fermentation, Halogens & Stoichiometry

a. (i) What is fermentation?

Fermentation is the chemical process where large organic molecules are broken down into simpler substances through the action of enzymes produced by microorganisms like yeast.

a. (ii) Show how ethanol can be produced from starch (equations only).

Starch to Maltose: $2(C_6H_{10}O_5)_n + nH_2O \xrightarrow{\text{Diastase}} nC_{12}H_{22}O_{11}$

Maltose to Glucose: $C_{12}H_{22}O_{11} + H_2O \xrightarrow{\text{Maltase}} 2C_6H_{12}O_6$

Glucose to Ethanol: $C_6H_{12}O_6 \xrightarrow{\text{Zymase}} 2C_2H_5OH + 2CO_2$

c. (i) Moles in 25 g of MgCO₃?

Molar Mass of MgCO₃ = $24 + 12 + (3 \times 16) = 84 \, \text{g/mol}$

Moles = $\frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Molar Mass}} = \frac{25}{84} = 0.298 \, \text{mol}$

c. (ii) Suitable indicator for strong acid/weak base titration?

Methyl orange.

c. (iii) Examples of oxides.

Acidic: SO₂, Neutral: CO.

c. (iv) Difference between hydrophobic and hydrophilic?

Hydrophilic compounds are soluble in water, while hydrophobic compounds are insoluble.

c. (v) IUPAC Names.

I. CH₃CH₂COOH: Propanoic acid
II. CH₃CH₂CH₂OH: Propan-1-ol

d. (i) Define reaction rate.

The change in concentration of a reactant or product per unit time.

d. (ii) Factors affecting reaction rate.

Temperature, Concentration, Catalyst.

2. Acids, Organics & Periodicity

a. (i) What is an acid anhydride?

A non-metallic oxide that reacts with water to form an acid.

a. (ii) Examples of weak acids.

Ethanoic acid (CH₃COOH), Carbonic acid (H₂CO₃).

a. (iii) Difference between fats and oils.

At room temperature, fats are solids while oils are liquids.

b. (i) Structures of compounds.

I. Hydroxybenzene (Phenol)

II. 1,3-dimethylbenzene

b. (ii) Physical properties of ethyne.

Colorless gas, insoluble in water.

b. (iii) Volume of 0.5 M H₂SO₄ containing 3.5 g of acid.

Molar Mass of H₂SO₄ = $98 \, \text{g/mol}$

Moles = $\frac{3.5}{98} = 0.0357 \, \text{mol}$

Volume = $\frac{\text{Moles}}{\text{Concentration}} = \frac{0.0357}{0.5} = 0.0714 \, \text{dm}^3 = 71.4 \, \text{cm}^3$

c. (i) State the periodic law.

The properties of elements are a periodic function of their atomic numbers.

3. Thermodynamics, Equilibria & Properties

a. (i) Define entropy.

A measure of the degree of disorder or randomness in a system.

a. (ii) Calculate the vapour density of oxygen.

Molar Mass = $\frac{0.4 \, \text{g}}{0.28 \, \text{dm}^3} \times 22.4 \, \text{dm}^3/\text{mol} = 32 \, \text{g/mol}$

Vapour Density = $\frac{\text{Molar Mass}}{2} = \frac{32}{2} = 16$

b. (i) For $N_2(g) + O_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2NO(g)$ with $\Delta H = +ve$, how to increase yield?

Increase the temperature. This favors the forward, endothermic reaction.

b. (ii) Two uses for each...

I. H₂SO₄: Fertilizers, dehydrating agent.
II. Diamond: Jewelry, cutting tools.
III. CO₂: Fire extinguishers, carbonated drinks.

b. (iii) Examples of normal salts.

Sodium chloride (NaCl), Potassium nitrate (KNO₃).

c. (i) Physical properties of teflon.

High melting point, non-stick (low friction).

c. (ii) Name CH₃-CH(CH₃)-CH(OH)-CH₂-CH₃.

2-methylpentan-3-ol.

c. (iii) Ways to prevent corrosion.

Painting, Sacrificial protection (galvanizing).

c. (iv) Allotrope of Carbon that conducts electricity.

Graphite.

d. (i) Basicity of ethanoic acid.

1 (monobasic).

d. (ii) Examples of electrolytes.

I. Strong: NaCl(aq)
II. Weak: CH₃COOH(aq)

4. Industrial Chemistry & Stoichiometry

a. (i) What is reforming?

Converting low-octane straight-chain hydrocarbons into high-octane branched or aromatic compounds.

a. (ii) Process involved in reforming.

Isomerization.

a. (iii) Benefits of cracking.

Produces more petrol; produces alkenes for the chemical industry.

b. (i) Flame test colours.

I. Sodium: Intense golden-yellow.
II. Calcium: Brick-red.

b. (ii) Mass of 0.50 mole of Calcium.

Mass = $0.50 \, \text{mol} \times 40 \, \text{g/mol} = 20 \, \text{g}$.

b. (iii) Moles in 14 g of nitrogen gas (N₂).

Moles = $\frac{14 \, \text{g}}{28 \, \text{g/mol}} = 0.5 \, \text{mol}$.

c. (i) Constant parameters for gas laws.

I. Charles' Law: Pressure.
II. Graham's Law: Temperature and Pressure.

c. (ii) Separation methods.

I. Chalk in water: Filtration.
II. Petroleum: Fractional distillation.

c. (iii) Industry using fractional distillation.

Petroleum refining industry.

c. (iv) Uses of crude oil products.

I. Jet fuel: Kerosene.
II. Heavy machinery fuel: Diesel.
III. Pipe coating: Bitumen.

d. (i) Drying agent for ammonia.

Calcium oxide (CaO).

d. (ii) Formula for elements X (valency 2) and Y (valency 1).

XY₂

d. (iii) Two types of plastics.

Thermoplastics (e.g., polythene), Thermosetting plastics (e.g., Bakelite).
